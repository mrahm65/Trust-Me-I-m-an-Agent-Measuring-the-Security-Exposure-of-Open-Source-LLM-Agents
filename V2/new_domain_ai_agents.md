## Key Findings

1. **Insurance** has the highest concentration of specialized agents (16+), particularly in claims processing with multi-agent architectures for fraud detection, policy verification, and risk assessment.

2. **Manufacturing / Industry 4.0** shows strong adoption (14 agents) with predictive maintenance being the dominant use case.

3. **Climate / Environment** and **Agriculture** are well-represented (14 and 11 agents respectively), reflecting growing interest in AI for sustainability.

4. **Social Good / NGO** has surprisingly strong representation (12 agents), particularly in disaster response.

5. **Space / Astronomy**, **Maritime / Naval**, and **Mining / Resources** have the fewest agents, indicating relatively unexplored domains with significant opportunity for new development.

6. **Government / Public Sector** has limited open-source agent implementations, suggesting this domain is still emerging.

7. Many agents use **LangGraph** as the primary framework for multi-agent orchestration, followed by **LangChain** and **CrewAI**.

8. **Multi-agent architectures** are prevalent across all domains, with most implementations using 3-5 specialized agents coordinated by a master/orchestrator agent.
